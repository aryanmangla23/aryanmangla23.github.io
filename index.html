Aryan Mangla

I’m a B.S. student in Neuroscience at The University of Texas at Austin, concurrently pursuing a Pre-Health Professions Certificate. My academic journey is driven by a long-term goal to pursue an MD-PhD, focusing on the integration of cognitive neuroengineering and systems neuroscience to develop closed-loop neuromodulation and brain-computer interface (BCI) technologies for disorders of cognitive control.

Currently, I conduct computational neuroscience and psychiatry research in the Center for Perceptual Systems with Dr. Thomas Elston. My work centers on investigating attentional filtering and value-based decision-making—processes often disrupted in psychiatric disorders. My foundational research at the Waggoner Center for Alcohol and Addiction Research, under Dr. Dayne Mayfield, focused on the molecular mechanisms of Alcohol Use Disorder. I designed and validated an AAV5-miRNA construct to selectively knock down Slc1a3 in the dorsomedial PFC, utilizing spatial transcriptomics, RNAScope, and behavioral paradigms to map astrocyte-driven neuroadaptations. Concurrently, I investigated at the College of Natural Sciences under Dr. Soo-Hyun Yang, the functional impact of cancer-associated mutations in the PARP1 gene to probe its role in DNA repair and disease, with implications for targeted therapies in both oncology and neurology.
